LYRA

A planetarium for your sound.

A runtime, GPU driven music visualizer for Windows. Press play on anything — LYRA turns it into a living visualisation that moves with every beat.

What it does

Two universes

LYRA listens to your system audio — so it reacts to anything you play — and renders up to a million GPU particles that impulse, swirl, and erupt in sync with the music.

The Nebula

A million-particle stellar nursery that breathes with the bass and ignites protostars on the beat. Change the track and it detonates a supernova.

The Supercluster

A face-on spiral galaxy with volumetric dust arms and a vortexing core. Track changes send a density wave through it; the hardest drops fire an impulse shockwave.

It hears everything

Reacts to your system audio from any source. Connect Spotify and it shows the song, the artist, and recolors the whole scene to match the album art.

Requirements

The visualizer runs on any audio. The Spotify extras need a Spotify Premium account.

Windows 10 / 11 — 64-bit. Ships as a portable LYRA.exe ⫘⫘⫘⫘⫘⫘
A WebGPU-capable GPU. Built on an NVIDIA RTX class card; lighter hardware can drop to a lower particle tier in-app.
Spotify Premium — for now-playing info and per-album color. Your login stays on your machine, encrypted with the OS keystore.
